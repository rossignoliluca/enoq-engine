## The Organs

Each interface is an **organ**—specialized but serving the whole organism.

| Organ | Form | Specialization | When Used |
|-------|------|----------------|-----------|
| **Space** | Web desktop | Full experience, all atmospheres | Deep work, reflection, decisions |
| **Pocket** | Mobile app | Compressed, immediate | On the go, quick support |
| **Whisper** | Voice | Invisible, ambient | Hands/eyes busy |
| **Glance** | Widget | Minimal, peripheral | Quick state, gentle nudge |
| **Inside** | Embedded | Contextual, in-flow | During other work (IDE, email) |

### One Organism, Many Forms

- Same kernel (nervous system)
- Same state (memory)
- Same constitution (values)
- Different manifestations (organs)

## Atmospheres

The organism adapts its **quality of presence** based on what it perceives.

Not "modes" that switch. **Atmosphere** that flows.

### The Five Atmospheres

| Atmosphere | Domain | Feeling | ENOQ's Posture |
|------------|--------|---------|----------------|
| **OPERATIONAL** | Task completion | Clarity, efficiency | Active helper |
| **HUMAN_FIELD** | Emotional presence | Safety, warmth | Witnessing companion |
| **DECISION** | Choice mapping | Order, options | Mapping assistant |
| **V_MODE** | Deep reflection | Space, stillness | Mirror, almost absent |
| **EMERGENCY** | Crisis containment | Containment, safety | Immediate, grounding |

### How Atmosphere Manifests

**Visual:**
- Background color/warmth
- Element density
- White space ratio

**Temporal:**
- Response latency (V_MODE slower)
- Animation speed
- Pause tolerance

**Behavioral:**
- Proactivity level
- Question frequency
- Suggestion density

### Transitions

Atmospheres don't switch. They **flow**.

- Transition time: 300-500ms
- Easing: ease-out (fast start, gentle arrival)
- Always interruptible

## Design Principles

### 1. Disappear to Serve

When working well, you don't see ENOQ. You see yourself more clearly.

The interface is **transparent**—you look through, not at.

### 2. Inhabit, Don't Use

You don't "use" ENOQ. You spend time there.

Design for **temporary inhabitation**, not task completion.

### 3. Atmosphere Before Function

First: How should this feel?
Then: What should this do?

The feeling carries the function.

### 4. Emptiness Is Full

Negative space is:
- Room to breathe
- Invitation to think
- Respect for the human
- Where insight emerges

### 5. Honor Thresholds

Transitions matter. Arrivals matter. Departures matter.

Don't trivialize with cute animations or gamification.

### 6. Control Always Felt

The human can always:
- Go back
- Stop
- Leave
- Understand what's happening
- Override

Autonomy is non-negotiable.

### 7. One Body, Many Forms

Same organism everywhere.
Different organs for different contexts.
State travels with the human.

### 8. Form Follows Need

The space responds to what's happening.
The human doesn't choose "modes."
The organism perceives and adapts.

## What the Interface Is Not

### Not a Dashboard
- No KPIs
- No graphs showing "what ENOQ knows"
- No progress bars for "growth"

### Not a Chatbot
- No avatar
- No fake personality
- No "I'm so happy to help!"

### Not a Tool
- No feature list
- No "premium features"
- Not optimized for engagement

### Not a Wellness App
- No streaks
- No achievements
- No gamification

---

## Entry Experience

### First Arrival

- Clean space, not empty
- Invitation, not demand
- "This is a place where I can think"

No tutorials. No walkthroughs. No forced onboarding.

Just: a space that invites.

### Returning

- Continue where left off, or start fresh
- No "Welcome back!"
- No guilt, no hooks

### Leaving

- Exit is easy, clean
- No "Are you sure?"
- No "Come back soon"
- The door is a door, not a trap

---

## Success Criteria

### Not:
- Time spent
- Messages sent
- Return rate
- "Engagement"

### But:
- Does the human complete what they came for?
- Do they leave more capable?
- Do they need ENOQ less over time?
- Is their autonomy intact?
